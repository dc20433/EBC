Hike to Everest Base Camp

Day 12: Lukla - Ramechhap - Kathmandu

Around 5AM, through the thin walls I heard noises indicating many were up; knowing the seats would be tight if the airport reopened today, everyone wanted to be the first at the airport. I also packed, checked out without coffee or breakfast, and went straight to the airport.

Sky cleared and the departure lounge was packed. People stood shoulder-to-shoulder at the check-in counters. Words were circulating that there would be eight or nine flights today, but no official announcement was made. I noticed that the airport staff all seemed to be very respectful and responsive to a busy, white-haired Nepali senior, who looked kind since he often wore a smiley face. So, I tried to chat with him when he was near. I asked him whether this type of crowding was unique, or happened all the time? He said unique AND all the time. Next, I asked what would he do at times hardly any passenger passing through? He did not take that one. Once I saw three young backpackers complain to him that they had waited for 2 days to fly out. “We can’t go back to that teahouse for one more night…” uttering one of them, with large teardrops rolling down her cheeks. Later I joked with the senior that managing passengers’ tears was also an item on his job description. He gave me an amusing look.

The first six planes came and left in full; all the seats seemed to have been wholesaled to large tour groups. Seeing so many people still waiting, and I had no boarding priority because my flight was to be several days later, I started mulling over the proposal made by a tour guide who comforted me that if by 8:30AM his clients, whose international departures were three days away, still had not got on the plane, they would hike back to Kathmandu and I was welcome to join them. The trip entailed one day of a long hike, followed by two days of jeep-ride. (Someone also told me that after one day of the jeep-ride you would be so sore as if your bones were falling apart.) Just when despair was about to seize on me, the 7th plane landed. The senior took my passport and handed it to the ticketing agent, who issued me a boarding pass. The senior explained to me there were no direct flights to Kathmandu today, and nobody knew whether the airport would be open tomorrow, so he flew me to Ramechhap, where I could catch a 5-hour airport shuttle back to Kathmandu. Seeing so many people still waiting without boarding passes, I couldn’t be more grateful. The senior barely heard my gratitude, threw me a smile and went back to his busy routines.

A half an hour later I was in the air off to Ramechhap. Kathmandu, you seemed so far away last night, here I come!
