Hike to Everest Base Camp

Day 4: Namche Bazaar - Tengboche

Today’s trail went up and down, but many more ups than downs. Gusting winds picked up dust and painted it everywhere, and it found its way into our mouths and noses, itching our eyes and throats. A rather uncomfortable day. But it was amazing that Mount Everest jumped out frequently from afar. It was easy to pick it out because it dragged a tail of dust cloud. Other magnificent high mountains were also seen clearly. Today was a major day to see Mount Everest, because in the following days until reaching the top of Mount Kalapattar (near EBC), the view of Mount Everest would be blocked by other high mountains.

We arrived at Tengboche (3,870m/12,697ft) after hiking for 6 or 7 hours and a brief stop for lunch. There is an impressive monastery tucked away in these high mountains, and the view is breathtaking. The glorious Mount Everest can be seen here on a clear day, but at this moment it was covered by clouds. It was rather disappointing to everyone.

After dinner, the chill inside the teahouse seemed to also deep-freeze the brains of its dwellers. But before retreating to my room, I still convinced my partner to change our planned route for tomorrow: he wanted to hike on a less crowded alternative route, but it would take one more day to reach EBC. I asked him whether such freezing conditions would be worth spending an extra night on. He thought for a second and gave me a thumbs up.
